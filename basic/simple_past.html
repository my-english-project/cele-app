01

Function & Use

When Do We Use the Simple Past?

Actions that started and finished at a specific time in the past — the action is over and not happening now.

The Simple Past is used to describe actions or situations that began and ended at a specific time in the past. The key idea is that the action is completely over — it has no connection to the present moment.
Key Concept: The action is finished. It is not happening now and it is not connected to now.
I visited my grandmother yesterday. (The visit is finished.)
Use 1 — Completed action at a specific time:
She arrived at the airport two hours ago.
Use 2 — Sequence of completed events:
He woke up, made coffee, and left for work.
Use 3 — Historical facts and past states:
Neil Armstrong walked on the moon in 1969.
Use 4 — Habits and repeated actions in the past:
When I was young, I played outside every afternoon.
CategoryTime Expressions
Yesterdayyesterday, yesterday morning, yesterday afternoon, the day before yesterday
Last…last night, last week, last month, last year, last Monday
…Agotwo days ago, five minutes ago, a year ago, a long time ago
Specific timein 2010, in the 1990s, on Monday, at 8 o'clock, in January
When…when I was a child, when she arrived, when it started
  • 01"I visited my grandmother yesterday. (The visit is finished.)"
  • 02"She woke up, made breakfast, and left for school."
  • 03"Neil Armstrong walked on the moon in 1969."
  • 04"When I was a child, I played soccer every weekend."
  • 05"The meeting started at 9 o'clock and finished at noon."

Special Verb

The Verb "To Be" in the Past

Two past forms: was and were — no auxiliary needed for negatives and questions.

WAS
I · He · She · It
WERE
You · We · They
SubjectPresentSimple Past
Iamwas
He / She / Itiswas
You / We / Theyarewere
✅ Affirmative  Subject + was/were + Complement
I was tired last night. / They were very happy.
❌ Negative  Subject + was/were + not + Complement
He wasn't at school. / They weren't hungry.
Contractions: was not = wasn't · were not = weren't
❓ Question  Was/Were + Subject + Complement?
Was she angry? → Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
Were they ready? → Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.
No auxiliary "did" — Unlike action verbs, "To Be" does NOT use "did" to form negatives or questions.
Did he was at home?
Was he at home?
Short answers — Always use was/were in short answers, never a contraction in the affirmative.
Was she happy? — Yes, she's.
Was she happy? — Yes, she was.
  • 01"I was tired last night." / "They were very happy with the results."
  • 02"She was a teacher in 2015." / "We were at home yesterday evening."
  • 03"He wasn't at school yesterday." / "The movie wasn't very good."
  • 04"Were you at the cinema last night? — Yes, I was."
  • 05"Was it cold yesterday? — No, it wasn't."

Action Verbs

Regular Verbs

Add -ed to the base form to create the Simple Past — same form for all subjects.

Regular verbs form the Simple Past by adding -ed (or just -d if the verb ends in -e) to the base form. This rule applies to all subjects — I, you, he, she, they — unlike many other languages.
Base FormSimple PastExample
playplayedI played soccer yesterday.
workworkedShe worked hard last week.
cookcookedHe cooked dinner for us.
wantwantedThey wanted pizza last night.
walkwalkedWe walked to the park this morning.
✅ Affirmative  Subject + Verb + -ed + Complement
I played soccer yesterday.
❌ Negative  Subject + didn't + Base Verb + Complement
I didn't play soccer yesterday.
I didn't played soccer.
❓ Question  Did + Subject + Base Verb + Complement?
Did you play soccer yesterday? → Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
  • 01"I played soccer yesterday." — affirmative
  • 02"She didn't work last Friday." — negative
  • 03"Did they visit the museum last month?" — question
  • 04"He cooked dinner for us three days ago."
  • 05"We walked to the park this morning."
04

Orthography

Spelling Rules for -ed

It is not always just adding "-ed" — five rules govern how the past form is written.

1

Verb ends in -e → just add -d

When the verb already ends in a silent -e, adding -ed would create a double vowel. We simply add -d.

live → lived  ·  love → loved  ·  smile → smiled  ·  dance → danced

2

CVC pattern (stressed final syllable) → double consonant + -ed

When a one-syllable verb — or a multi-syllable verb stressed on the last syllable — ends in Consonant-Vowel-Consonant, double the final consonant.

stop → stopped  ·  plan → planned  ·  admit → admitted  ·  refer → referred

3

CVC pattern (stress on 1st syllable) → do NOT double

Two-syllable verbs that end in CVC but are stressed on the first syllable do not double the final consonant.

happen → happened  ·  enter → entered  ·  offer → offered  ·  suffer → suffered

4

Ends in -w, -x, or vowel+-y → just add -ed

Verbs ending in -w, -x, or a vowel followed by -y never double — simply add -ed.

fix → fixed  ·  enjoy → enjoyed  ·  snow → snowed  ·  play → played

5

Consonant + -y → change -y to -ied

When a verb ends in a consonant + y, remove the -y and add -ied. But if it ends in a vowel + y, just add -ed (Rule 4).

try → tried  ·  carry → carried  ·  study → studied  ·  worry → worried

Action Verbs — No Rule

Irregular Verbs

These verbs do not follow the -ed rule — they change form completely and must be memorized.

⚠️ Warning: Irregular verbs do NOT follow the -ed rule. They change their form completely in the past. There is no shortcut — you must memorize them!
The good news: irregular verbs only change in the affirmative. In negatives and questions, you still use did + base form — the same rule as regular verbs.

Sentence Form

Negative Sentences

Use did not (didn't) + base verb — the main verb never takes the past form in negatives.

Formula
Subject + did not (didn't) + Base Verb + Complement
Critical Point: The auxiliary did carries the past meaning. The main verb stays in its base form.
She didn't played soccer. / He didn't went home.
She didn't play soccer. / He didn't go home.
TypeAffirmativeNegative
Regular verbShe played soccer.She didn't play soccer.
Irregular verbHe went to the park.He didn't go to the park.
To BeThey were happy.They weren't happy.
Regular verbI worked yesterday.I didn't work yesterday.
To Be is different! It uses wasn't / weren't — no "did" needed.
She didn't be at school.
She wasn't at school.
Mistake 1 — Using past form after didn't:
I didn't watched TV last night.
I didn't watch TV last night.
Mistake 2 — Using "didn't" with To Be:
He didn't be at the meeting.
He wasn't at the meeting.
Mistake 3 — Double negation:
I didn't eat nothing.
I didn't eat anything.
  • 01"She didn't call me yesterday." (regular)
  • 02"He didn't go to the concert." (irregular)
  • 03"They weren't ready for the exam." (to be)
  • 04"I didn't have breakfast this morning."
  • 05"We didn't finish the project on time."

Interrogative Form

Questions in the Simple Past

Two types: Yes/No questions for confirmation, Wh- questions for specific information.

Formula
Did + Subject + Base Verb + Complement + ?
QuestionShort Answer ✅Short Answer ❌
Did you study?Yes, I did.No, I didn't.
Did she call you?Yes, she did.No, she didn't.
Did they go to the concert?Yes, they did.No, they didn't.
Formula
Wh-word + did + Subject + Base Verb + ?
Question WordQuestionExample Answer
WhatWhat did you do yesterday?I watched a film.
WhereWhere did he go last weekend?He went to the mountains.
WhenWhen did they arrive?They arrived at noon.
WhyWhy did she leave early?Because she was tired.
HowHow did you spend your holiday?I traveled to the coast.
WhoWho did you meet at the party?I met my old teacher.
To Be questions invert subject and verb — no "did" needed:
Was/Were + Subject + Complement?

Was she happy? → Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't.
Were they at the party? → Yes, they were. / No, they weren't.
Wh- + To BeExample
Where was…?Where was she yesterday? — She was at the library.
Why were…?Why were they late? — Because the bus broke down.
How was…?How was the concert? — It was amazing!
Mistake 1 — Using past form after did:
Did you saw the film?
Did you see the film?
Mistake 2 — Using "did" with To Be:
Did she was at school?
Was she at school?
Mistake 3 — Wrong word order in Wh- questions:
Where you went last weekend?
Where did you go last weekend?
  • 01"Did you study? — Yes, I did. / When did you study? — I studied last night."
  • 02"Where did you study? — I studied at the library."
  • 03"Did she call you yesterday? — No, she didn't."
  • 04"Was he happy with the results? — Yes, he was."
  • 05"Why did they leave the party early? — Because they were tired."
