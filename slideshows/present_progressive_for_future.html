01 · Concept

What is a Future Arrangement?

An arrangement is a plan that has already been decided — usually involving another person or an external commitment.

The Present Continuous is used to talk about future arrangements. An arrangement is something that is done to prepare or plan for something in the future. It is also an agreement between two people or groups about something that will happen.
Formula: Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing + complement + time expression
"I am having a party tonight."  ·  "She is starting an English course next summer."
Important: The arrangement must already be fixed or confirmed. If you are just thinking about something, use going to or will instead.
The key question to ask yourself
"Have I already arranged this
with someone else?"
Yes → use Present Progressive for future
"I am meeting Ana tomorrow." (We already agreed on a time and place.)
No → use will or going to
"I think I will call her later." (No specific arrangement yet.)
  • 01"I'm having a party tonight. (Some friends have already been invited.)"
  • 02"Sue is starting an English course next summer. (She has already registered.)"
  • 03"We are flying to Toronto next week. (Tickets are booked.)"
  • 04"He is meeting the doctor on Friday. (Appointment confirmed.)"
  • 05"They are arriving at 8 PM. (We already know their flight time.)"

02 · Time Markers

Future Time Expressions

These expressions signal that the Present Progressive is being used for a future plan, not a current action.

Common future time expressions used with Present Progressive:
tonighttomorrow this weekendnext Monday next Fridaynext Saturday next weeknext weekend next yearnext summer on Saturdayin the morning
Key contrast: Without a future time expression, the same sentence describes what is happening right now.
"She is cooking." → happening NOW
"She is cooking dinner tomorrow." → future PLAN
Sentence Meaning
"I am leaving." Happening right now
"I am leaving tonight." Future arrangement
"She is working." Happening at this moment
"She is working next week." Future plan
"They are flying." In progress now
"They are flying on Friday." Booked arrangement
  • 01"I'm visiting my grandmother this weekend."
  • 02"They are leaving for Spain next Monday."
  • 03"He is arriving tomorrow morning."
  • 04"We are going out tonight — dinner is booked."
  • 05"She is coming to the meeting next Friday."

04 · Grammar Structures

Affirmative, Negative & Questions

The same Present Progressive structures apply — the only difference is the future time expression that anchors the sentence.

Affirmative Formula
Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + complement + time expression
  • 01"I'm staying with Susan this weekend."
  • 02"Jamie and his family are flying to Toronto next week."
  • 03"She is starting an English course next summer."
Negative Formula
Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing + complement + time expression
  • 01"She is not doing her homework after dinner." (plan changed)
  • 02"Julie is not leaving Toronto tomorrow." (cancelled)
  • 03"We aren't meeting on Monday — we moved it to Wednesday."
The negative often implies a change of plans or cancellation of something that was arranged.
Question Formula
Am/Is/Are + Subject + verb-ing + complement + time expression + ?
  • 01"Are Jamie and his family flying to Toronto next week?"
  • 02"What are we having for dinner tonight?"
  • 03"Is she coming to the party on Saturday?"
  • 04"Where are you going next weekend?"
Form Structure Example
✅ Affirmative Subject + is/are + verb-ing + time She is leaving on Friday.
❌ Negative Subject + isn't/aren't + verb-ing + time She isn't leaving on Friday.
❓ Yes/No Q. Is/Are + subject + verb-ing + time? Is she leaving on Friday?
❓ Wh- Q. Wh- + is/are + subject + verb-ing? When is she leaving?

05 · Comparison

Present Progressive vs Going To

Both express future intentions — but there is a key difference in how confirmed or specific the plan is.

Present Progressive
Fixed arrangement — confirmed with another person or booked in advance.
"I'm meeting Ana at 6." (She knows, we agreed.)
Going to
Intention or plan — decided but not necessarily arranged with others.
"I'm going to call her later." (My intention — not confirmed with her yet.)
Feature Present Progressive Going to
Type of future Fixed arrangement Intention / plan
Other person involved? Usually yes Not necessarily
Level of certainty Very high — already set High — decided
Typical signal tonight, next Monday… soon, later, one day…
Example "We are having dinner with the Smiths on Saturday." "We're going to have a party someday."
Mistake 1 — Using Present Progressive for vague intentions:
I am visiting Paris one day.
I'm going to visit Paris one day. (no specific arrangement)
Mistake 2 — Using will for confirmed arrangements:
I will meet you at the airport tonight.
I'm meeting you at the airport tonight. (already arranged)
Mistake 3 — Forgetting the time expression:
We are flying to Canada. (ambiguous — now or future?)
We are flying to Canada next Thursday. (clear future plan)

— End of Presentation

Keep practising!

Use the Present Progressive for confirmed arrangements — and always include a future time expression to make the meaning clear.
